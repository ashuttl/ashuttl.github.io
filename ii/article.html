Connecting Remote Communities to the World

Rural coastal communities strategize to build broadband networks that will provide access and opportunities for young and old.

Written by Ellsworth Fowler
With data journalism by Norman Beaupré, Anne Ford, Daphne Plymale, and Roger Leavitt
Posted June 19, 2019        Last updated January 22, 2020

Lack of access to fast, reliable, and affordable internet service has long been a leading factor working against the sustainability of small-town life on the coast of Maine. Rural towns with poor internet service are finding it difficult to attract young families. Businesses are unlikely to locate where internet service is spotty, and senior residents increasingly rely on dependable communications networks to support their ability to age in place.
