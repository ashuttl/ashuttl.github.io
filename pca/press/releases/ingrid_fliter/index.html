Fiery, passionate, and evocative, Argentine pianist Ingrid Fliter to perform Beethoven, Schubert, and Chopin at Merrill on Wednesday, February 6  

Pre-curtain talk explores pianist’s program and her coveted Gilmore Artist Award

PORTLAND – Ingrid Fliter, the fiery young Argentine pianist poised for a major international career, will perform works by Franz Schubert, Ludwig von Beethoven, and Frédéric Chopin in a solo recital at Merrill Auditorium on Wednesday, February 6 at 7:30 pm. The concert by Fliter kicks off the 2008 series of classical music performances presented by PCA Great Performances.

Fliter, a thirty-five-year-old native of Argentina now living in Milan, is the most recent winner of the $300,000 Gilmore Artist Award, known as the “stealth” award because the artists do not know they have been nominated or are being judged. Fliter is the first woman to receive this highly prized honor.  Her playing is fiery, passionate, and evocative, flecked with imagination that shows both a fertile mind and phenomenal technique at work.

The program for Fliter’s PCA-presented recital features Schubert’s Impromptu Op 90 (Nos 1 and 2), Beethoven’s Sonata No. 31 in A-flat Major (Op 110), and Chopin’s Nocturne Op 27 No 2 and Sonata No 3 in B minor (Op 58).

The London Daily Telegraph says “music [seems to] flow from [Fliter] with an utterly natural lyrical impulse… A fusion of poetry and dynamism.”

A free, pre-curtain lecture by Dr. David Ober will be offered in the Merrill Auditorium Green Room before the performance at 6 pm. The lecture, entitled “Passionate about the Piano,” will offer insights into Ingrid Fliter’s program for the evening and also explain how musicians are chosen to receive the coveted Gilmore Artist Award.

Tickets for the Ingrid Fliter performance are priced at $38. Student tickets are just $10. Tickets are available through PortTix, the box office at Merrill Auditorium; by phone at 207.842.0800; and at pcagreatperformances.org. Information about group rates is available through the PCA web site or by calling 207.773.3150.

High resolution photos are available for download from our press room.

Since 1931, PCA Great Performances has brought high-quality performing artists to southern Maine, including classical and popular music, jazz, opera, dance, theater, and Broadway performances. In addition to live performances, we bring the exhilaration of the performing arts out into our community through educational and outreach programs called PCA Offstage. Formerly known as the Portland Concert Association, we are a nonprofit organization dedicated to bringing Maine the world.
