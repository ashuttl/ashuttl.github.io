Dancer-illusionists Momix to open PCA Great Performances’ 2008 dance card with sold-out Lunar Sea at Merrill on Thursday, January 24  

Legendary Limón Dance Company, Ronald K. Brown/Evidence follow in February, April; all three dance events include PCA Offstage classes, community activities

PORTLAND – The dancer-illusionists of Momix return to Merrill Auditorium for a performance of the “eye-popping and mind-boggling” (New York Times) new work, Lunar Sea, on Thursday, January 24 at 7:30 pm.

Momix’s performance of Lunar Sea kicks off PCA Great Performances’ 2008 schedule of contemporary dance events at Merrill. The January show by Momix will be followed by a performance featuring the Limón Dance Company, one of the most enduring and influential ensembles in dance, on Wednesday, February 27 at 7:30 pm. Choreographer Ronald K. Brown and his company, Evidence, cap off PCA’s winter and spring dance card, bringing their rich blend of African, modern, ballet, and hip-hop stylings to Merrill on Wednesday, April 9, also at 7:30 pm.

The Momix performance on January 24 are sold out. Tickets for the performances by the Limón company and Ronald K. Brown/Evidence are plentiful. There are still openings for masterclasses and other PCA Offstage educational activities associated with all three upcoming dance performances.

In Lunar Sea, the new, evening-length work fashioned by Momix founder and choreographer Moses Pendleton, the highly athletic, supercharged dance ensemble creates surrealistic images using breathtaking movement, light, shadow, music, costume, props, and humor. Lunar Sea is an extraterrestrial spectacle featuring phosphorescent dancers who appear to defy gravity as if dancing on the moon.

Momix ticket holders are invited to “A Journey to the Moon’s Lunar Seas,” a free lecture-demonstration by Dr. Jerry LaSala, professor of physics and astronomy and director of the Southworth Planetarium at the University of Southern Maine in Portland.  The free presentation at the Southworth is set for Tuesday, January 22 at 7:30 pm. Prof. LaSala will lead an interdisciplinary exploration of the moonscape, featuring atmospheric music and examining the heavens as an age-old inspiration for “lunatics,” artists, and scientists. The presentation is also free to USM students, faculty, and staff with an ID. Admission to the event for non-Momix ticket holders and non-USM community members is $5 for adults and $4 for students and seniors.

A member of the Momix company will also offer a dance masterclass with Momix for intermediate dancers on Friday, January 25 from 10 to 11:30 am at the Portland Arts and Technology High School, 196 Allen Avenue in Portland. The class includes a warmup, repertory phrases, and guided improvisation. Participants are encouraged to “bring physicality and a sense of humor – and be prepared for an adventure!” The class fee is $20; if space permits, interested participants may audit the class for $10. Space is limited and early registration is advised. Call 207.773.3150 or visit PCA Offstage online for details.

PCA Great Performances will provide more information about the upcoming Jose Limón Dance Company and Ronald K. Brown/Evidence performances at a later date. If you would like to learn more about these two performances and related PCA Offstage activities now, visit the PCA website at pcagreatperformances.org.

High-resolution photos of Momix, the Limón company, and Ronald K. Brown/Evidence are available for download from our press room.

Since 1931, PCA Great Performances has brought high-quality performing artists to southern Maine, including classical and popular music, jazz, opera, dance, theater, and Broadway performances. In addition to live performances, we bring the exhilaration of the performing arts out into our community through educational and outreach programs called PCA Offstage. Formerly known as the Portland Concert Association, we are a nonprofit organization dedicated to bringing Maine the world.
